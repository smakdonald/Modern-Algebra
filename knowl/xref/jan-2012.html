Exercise Group D.6.1–9. January 2012.

1. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a (not necessarily finite) group and \(H\) and \(K\) normal subgroups such that \(G=H K\text{.}\) Prove that \(G /(H \cap K) \cong G / H \times G / K\text{.}\)
Solution.

2. Problem 2.

Suppose \(G\) is a finite group which has precisely one subgroup of order \(d\) for each divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\text{.}\) Prove that \(G\) is cyclic.
Solution.

3. Problem 3.

Let \(G\) be a group of order \(90=2 \cdot 3^{2} \cdot 5\) and let \(\operatorname{Syl}_{3}(G)\) denote the set of Sylow 3-subgroups of \(G\text{.}\)
  1. Suppose for any \(Q, Q^{\prime} \in \operatorname{Syl}_{3}(G)\) either \(Q=Q^{\prime}\) or \(Q \cap Q^{\prime}=\{1\}\text{.}\) Prove that \(G\) is not simple.
  2. Suppose there exists \(Q, Q^{\prime} \in \operatorname{Syl}_{3}(G)\) such that \(\left|Q \cap Q^{\prime}\right|=3\text{.}\) Prove that \(G\) is not simple. (Hint: Consider the normalizer of \(Q \cap Q^{\prime}\text{.}\))
Solution.

4. Problem 4.

Let \(F\) be a field of characteristic \(p>0, a \in F\text{,}\) and consider the polynomial \(f(x)=x^{p}-x-a \in F[x]\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that \(f(x)\) is either irreducible over \(F\) or splits into linear factors over \(F\text{.}\) (Hint: If \(\alpha\) is a root of \(f(x)\) in some splitting field, consider \(\alpha+i\) for \(i \in \mathbb{F}_{p}\text{,}\) the field of \(p\) elements.)
  2. Suppose \(f(x)\) is irreducible over \(F\text{.}\) Prove that the Galois group of \(f(x)\) over \(F\) is cyclic.
Solution.

5. Problem 5.

Let \(\alpha=\sqrt{2}+\sqrt{5} \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
  1. Find the minimal polynomial \(f(x)\) of \(\alpha\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(E\) be the splitting field of \(f(x)\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\) Find the Galois group \(G\) of \(E / \mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
  3. Find all subgroups of \(G\) and generators for the corresponding intermediate fields of \(E / \mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
Solution.

6. Problem 6.

Let \(F\) be any field, and let \(x, y\text{,}\) and \(t\) be indeterminates. Prove in detail that \(F[x, y] /\left(y^{2}-x^{3}\right)\) and \(F\left[t^{2}, t^{3}\right]\) are isomorphic rings.
Solution.

7. Problem 7.

Consider the following matrix over the complex numbers:
\begin{equation*} A=\left[\begin{array}{cccc} -1 & -9 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 5 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 7 & 2 & 0 \\ 4 & 13 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
  1. Show that the characteristic polynomial of \(A\) is \((t-2)^{4}\text{.}\)
  2. Find the Jordan canonical form \(J\) of \(A\text{.}\)
  3. Find an invertible matrix \(P\) such that \(P^{-1} A P=J\text{.}\)
Solution.

8. Problem 8.

Let \(N\) be a submodule of an \(R\)-module \(M\text{.}\) Using Zorn’s Lemma, prove that there is a submodule \(N^{\prime}\) such that
  • \(N \cap N^{\prime}=(0)\text{,}\) and
  • \(N^{\prime \prime} \cap\left(N+N^{\prime}\right) \neq(0)\) for every non-zero submodule \(N^{\prime \prime}\) of \(M\text{.}\)
Solution.

9. Problem 9.

Let \(A\) be a square matrix over the field \(\mathbb{C}\) of complex numbers.
  1. Suppose \(A\) is invertible. Prove that there is a square matrix \(B\) over \(\mathbb{C}\) such that \(B^{2}=A\text{.}\) (Hint: Reduce to the case that \(A=I+N\) where \(I\) is the identity matrix and \(N\) is a nilpotent matrix.)
  2. Show by example that (a) can fail if \(A\) is not assumed to be invertible.
Solution.
