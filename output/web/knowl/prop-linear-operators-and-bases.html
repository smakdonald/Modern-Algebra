Let \(R\) be a non-zero commutative ring. Given a linear operator \(g: V \to V\) on a free \(R\)-module \(V\) of finite rank \(n\text{,}\) we have: 1. The matrices representing \(g\) with respect to any two choices of bases of \(V\) are similar 2. If \(X\) represents \(g\) with respect to a basis \(B\) of \(V\) and if \(Y\) is similar to \(X\text{,}\) then there is a basis \(C\) of \(V\) such that the matrix representing \(g\) with respect to \(C\) is \(Y\text{.}\)
Proof..
We proved the first assertion above.
Say \(X = [g]_B^B\) and \(Y = PXP^{-1}\) for some invertible matrix \(P\text{.}\) By Proposition above, \(P = [\id_V]_B^C\) for a (unique) new basis \(C\text{.}\) So \(Y = [\id]^C_{B} [g]_B^B [\id_V]_C^{B} = [g]_{C}^C\text{.}\)
